# 🪔 Diwali Sales Analysis with Python – A Retail EDA Project

This project performs an in-depth **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on a Diwali sales dataset using Python. The goal is to uncover customer insights, purchasing behavior, and product trends to help retailers make data-driven decisions during the festive season.

---

## 📌 Problem Statement

The retail company wants to analyze customer demographics and product trends during Diwali to improve targeting, boost sales, and optimize inventory management for future festive seasons.

---

## 🧠 Objectives

- Understand customer demographics (gender, age, occupation, region)
- Identify top-performing product categories and sales zones
- Uncover patterns in order frequency and spending behavior
- Provide actionable insights using visualizations and analysis

---

## 📁 Dataset Overview

- **Filename**: `Diwali Sales Data.csv`
- **Records**: 11,251
- **Key Columns**:
  - `Gender`, `Age Group`, `Marital_Status`
  - `State`, `Zone`, `Occupation`
  - `Product_Category`, `Orders`, `Amount`

> 🟡 Some columns with no data (`Status`, `unnamed1`) were dropped during preprocessing.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used

- Python
- Jupyter Notebook
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn

---

## 📊 Key Questions Explored

- Which gender contributes most to Diwali sales?
- Which age group has the highest purchase count and revenue?
- Do married customers shop more during Diwali?
- Which states and zones are most profitable?
- Which occupations drive the highest purchases?
- What are the most popular product categories?
- How many items do customers typically buy?

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- Female customers aged **26–35** make the most purchases.
- **Married professionals** contribute a large share of revenue.
- **Top states**: Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Karnataka.
- High sales in **Food, Clothing, Electronics** categories.
- Majority of customers made **multiple orders**.
